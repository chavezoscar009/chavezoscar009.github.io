Óscar A. Chávez Ortiz (He/Him/His)

A Final year Ph.D Candidate at The University of Texas at Austin.
My area of research revolves around understanding how Lyman-alpha
escapes from galaxies and its connection to galaxy properties
to better understand and improve Lyman-alpha based
reionization predictions. I leverage big-data analytics,
automated scripts, and ML frameworks to uncover the complex
mapping of galaxy properties to Lyman-alpha emission.

Research Focus

Understanding Lyman-alpha escape from galaxies to improve reionization predictions

I design and implement scalable data pipelines to process large observational datasets (≥1M records), engineer predictive features, and train machine learning models to infer key galaxy properties. My work emphasizes reproducibility, uncertainty quantification, and robust model evaluation, integrating Bayesian inference and probabilistic modeling to extract insights from diverse sets of astronomical data.

Machine Learning & Data Pipelines

Building predictive models and scalable workflows

I develop end-to-end pipelines for large datasets, including automated data cleaning, feature engineering, model training, hyperparameter optimization, and performance evaluation. I leverage scikit-learn, PyTorch, TensorFlow, and Keras for predictive modeling, and I apply Bayesian methods and MCMC sampling to quantify uncertainty and support robust scientific conclusions.

Projects & Applications

Applying ML to research and healthcare

Beyond astronomy, I have built a deep learning pipeline to classify audio files for auditory healthcare. This includes preprocessing raw audio into spectrograms, training neural networks from scratch, and leveraging transfer learning for improved classification. I evaluate model generalization using patient-level splits and carefully analyze performance to inform real-world applications.
